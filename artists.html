My Favorite Artists

These are my favorite Japanese music artists from my personal collection. From experimental rock to gentle R&B, each artist has a unique musical style and endless creativity.

01

King Gnu

キングヌー

Genre: Alternative Rock / J-pop

Formed: 2017

Members: 4

King Gnu is one of Japan's most innovative rock bands of the contemporary era. They perfectly blend classical music training with modern rock and electronic elements, creating a unique "Tokyo New Mixture Style." The lead vocalist Tsuchiya Daiki is also a collaborator and arranger for artists like Mizuki Genshi.

Representative Works

White Day Flying Boat BOY Chameleon
02

YOASOBI

ヨアソビ

Genre: J-pop / Electronic

Formed: 2019

Members: 2

YOASOBI is a musical duo composed of composer Ayase and vocalist ikura. Their unique creative method is adapting novels into songs, with each song telling a complete story. "Yoru ni Kakeru" brought them instant fame, becoming an iconic J-pop duo of the digital era.

Representative Works

Racing into the Night Idol Monster Navy Blue
03

向井太一

むかいたいち

Genre: R&B / Soul / J-pop

Formed: 2016

Members: 1

Taichi Mukai is a next-generation R&B artist in Japan, known for his delicate vocal tone and urban musical style. His music blends Western R&B and Soul with Japanese pop, creating a warm and profound listening experience.

Representative Works

Reset FLY BLUE Pure
04

Aimer

エメ

Genre: J-pop / Ballad / Rock

Formed: 2011

Members: 1

Aimer is renowned for her distinctive "raspy voice," often called the "most beautiful broken voice." Her music spans across pop, rock, electronic and various other styles. She has performed numerous anime theme songs and is one of Japan's most beloved female vocalists.

Representative Works

Echoes Katakoi Butterfly Knot SPARK-AGAIN
05

Zutto Mayonaka

ZUTOMAYO

Genre: J-pop / Rock / Electronic

Formed: 2018

Members: 1+

"Zutto Mayonaka de Iinoni" (abbreviated as ZUTOMAYO) is a music project centered around the mysterious vocalist ACAね. Known for its unique visual art, complex musical arrangements, and profound lyrics, it is a representative of Japan's "internet-era music."

Representative Works

Bite Down on the Second Hand Can't Be Correct Study More All of Those Guys at the Reunion
